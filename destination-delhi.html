Delhi

The Heart of India | Where Heritage Meets Modernity

Delhi is a vibrant metropolis where ancient history, diverse cultures, and modern life blend seamlessly. From majestic Mughal monuments to bustling markets and mouthwatering street food, Delhi offers an unforgettable experience for every traveler.

Discover Delhi Tours

Best Tourist Places in Delhi

  • Red Fort: Iconic Mughal fortress and UNESCO World Heritage Site, a symbol of India's rich past.
  • Qutub Minar: The world’s tallest brick minaret, an impressive example of Indo-Islamic architecture.
  • India Gate: A prominent war memorial and popular public gathering spot, especially in the evenings.
  • Humayun’s Tomb: A magnificent Mughal tomb, considered a precursor to the Taj Mahal.
  • Lotus Temple: A stunning Baháʼí House of Worship known for its unique, tranquil, flower-like architecture.
  • Akshardham Temple: A modern architectural marvel with intricate carvings, exhibitions, and serene gardens.

Travel Tips for Exploring Delhi

  • Best time to visit: October to March for pleasant weather, avoiding summer heat and monsoon rains.
  • Must-try local street food includes chaat, parathas, momos, and jalebi – explore Old Delhi for the best experience.
  • Utilize the efficient Delhi Metro for convenient and affordable travel across the city.
  • Respect local customs at religious sites: remove shoes, cover head (for women in some places), and dress modestly.
  • Stay hydrated, carry sunscreen, and a hat, especially when exploring historical sites outdoors.
  • Bargain wisely when shopping in local markets like Janpath or Sarojini Nagar.
