Agra

Home of the Taj Mahal | A Monument to Love

Step into the enchanting city of Agra, renowned worldwide for the magnificent Taj Mahal, a UNESCO World Heritage site and an eternal symbol of love. Beyond its iconic monument, Agra boasts a rich Mughal history, vibrant culture, and delicious cuisine.

Discover Agra Tours

Iconic Landmarks in Agra

  • Taj Mahal: The breathtaking white marble mausoleum, a wonder of the world and a testament to eternal love.
  • Agra Fort: A grand Mughal fortress, a UNESCO World Heritage site, and the imperial city of the Mughal rulers.
  • Fatehpur Sikri: A well-preserved deserted Mughal city, an architectural masterpiece near Agra.
  • Itmad-ud-Daula's Tomb (Baby Taj): A beautiful Mughal mausoleum, considered a draft for the Taj Mahal.
  • Mehtab Bagh: A charbagh complex with stunning views of the Taj Mahal across the Yamuna River, especially at sunset.
  • Akbar's Tomb, Sikandra: The impressive final resting place of the great Mughal Emperor Akbar.

Agra Travel Tips

  • Best time to visit: October to March for pleasant weather.
  • Visit the Taj Mahal at sunrise for fewer crowds and magical light.
  • Explore local markets for unique marble inlay work, leather goods, and handicrafts.
  • Be aware of touts and guides around major attractions; consider pre-booking official guides.
  • Try the famous Petha (sweet candy) and Mughlai cuisine.
  • Wear comfortable shoes, as there's a lot of walking involved at historical sites.
