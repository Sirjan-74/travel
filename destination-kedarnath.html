Kedarnath

Abode of Lord Shiva | Himalayan Pilgrimage

Nestled amidst the majestic Garhwal Himalayas, Kedarnath is one of the holiest Hindu shrines, part of the Char Dham pilgrimage. A journey to this sacred abode is a spiritual odyssey, offering breathtaking vistas, serene spiritual ambiance, and a profound connection with nature.

Embark on Your Pilgrimage

Sacred Sites & Attractions

  • Kedarnath Temple: The ancient and revered Shiva temple, standing tall against the Himalayan backdrop.
  • Bhairavnath Temple: Located near Kedarnath Temple, dedicated to Lord Bhairav, the protector deity.
  • Gandhi Sarovar (Chorabari Tal): A tranquil lake where Mahatma Gandhi's ashes were immersed, also known for a mythological connection to Shiva.
  • Vasuki Tal: A pristine high-altitude lake, offering mesmerizing views and a challenging trek from Kedarnath.
  • Adi Shankaracharya Samadhi: The resting place of the great 8th-century philosopher and saint, revered for unifying Hinduism.
  • Mandakini River: Witness the powerful and sacred Mandakini River, originating from Chorabari Glacier.

Travel Tips for Kedarnath Pilgrimage

  • Best time to visit: May to June (pre-monsoon) and September to October (post-monsoon) for clear weather.
  • Acclimatize properly; ascend gradually to avoid altitude sickness.
  • Carry warm, waterproof clothing, sturdy trekking shoes, and essential medicines.
  • Pony, Palki (doli), and Helicopter services are available for those unable to trek.
  • Respect the sanctity of the shrine and surrounding environment.
  • Book accommodations and travel arrangements well in advance, especially during peak season.
