Varanasi

Spiritual Heart of India | Where Eternity Flows

Varanasi is one of the world's oldest inhabited cities, a spiritual epicenter on the banks of the sacred Ganges. Experience timeless rituals, ancient temples, and the profound cycle of life and death in this holy city.

Discover Varanasi Tours

Best Tourist Places in Varanasi

  • Kashi Vishwanath Temple: One of the 12 Jyotirlingas, dedicated to Lord Shiva.
  • Dashashwamedh Ghat: Famous for the evening Ganga Aarti ceremony.
  • Sarnath: Where Lord Buddha gave his first sermon, a major Buddhist site.
  • Manikarnika Ghat: The main cremation ghat, symbolizing the cycle of life and death.
  • Ramnagar Fort: Historical fort with a museum showcasing royal artifacts.
  • Assi Ghat: Known for its morning yoga sessions and cultural performances.

Travel Tips for Exploring Varanasi

  • Best time to visit: October to March for pleasant weather, avoiding summer heat and monsoon rains.
  • Witness the Ganga Aarti at Dashashwamedh Ghat in the evening for a mesmerizing spiritual experience.
  • Take a boat ride on the Ganges at sunrise to see the city come alive.
  • Respect local customs at religious sites: remove shoes, dress modestly, and avoid photography where prohibited.
  • Stay hydrated and be cautious with street food; opt for recommended spots to try local delicacies like kachori and banarasi paan.
  • Be aware of crowds and keep valuables secure, especially at busy ghats.
